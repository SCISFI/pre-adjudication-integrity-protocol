# Weekly Content Template v1

Each week of The Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol must follow this structure.

## 1. Week Title and Orientation

Briefly name the focus of the week and why it matters.

## 2. Core Teaching

Direct teaching on the week’s recovery concept. Tone must be firm, sober, non-shaming, practical, direct, legally cautious, and recovery-focused.

## 3. Reality Check

Briefly confront common avoidance patterns such as minimization, image management, self-pity, blaming, secrecy, panic, court-performance thinking, and shame collapse.

## 4. Legal and Clinical Boundary Reminder

Use this standard reminder:

> Do not enter offense details, victim names, investigative facts, illegal content descriptions, police facts, or legal strategy. Use this space for recovery reflection, accountability, boundaries, emotions, and next right actions. Discuss legal details with your attorney and clinical details with your licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider.

## 5. Reflection Questions

Default recurring questions:

1. What truth am I facing this week?
2. Where am I tempted to hide, minimize, blame, or manage my image?
3. What emotion is most dangerous for me right now?
4. What boundary do I need to honor this week?
5. Who needs to know how I am really doing?
6. What do I need to discuss with my licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider?
7. What is my one integrity commitment this week?

## 6. Relationship / Family Reflection

Adapt based on relationship status. For married/cohabitating/separated users, focus on spouse/partner impact. For single/divorced/no active partner users, focus on family, household, child-safety concerns, future relational integrity, or community trust.

## 7. Therapist Prompt

Tell the participant what to bring to therapy.

## 8. Weekly Integrity Commitment

Prompt:

> What is one specific integrity commitment you will practice this week?

## 9. Completion Checkbox

Required wording:

> I completed this reflection honestly and without intentionally hiding what I know needs attention.

## 10. AI Feedback

AI feedback may respond after submission under the approved AI guardrails. It must not provide legal advice, therapy, risk assessment, diagnosis, offense-detail interpretation, or court-performance language.
