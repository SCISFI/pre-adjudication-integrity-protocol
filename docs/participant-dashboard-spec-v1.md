# Participant Dashboard Specification v1

## Purpose

The participant dashboard exists to provide structure, reflection access, daily check-ins, weekly commitments, and completion attestations.

It must not shame, score, monitor, threaten, predict, or imply legal benefit.

## Participant Dashboard May Show

- welcome message
- current week
- weekly lesson access
- weekly reflection form
- weekly integrity commitment
- completion checkbox
- daily check-in form
- prior completed weeks
- submitted / not submitted status
- reminder of no-offense-detail policy
- crisis language where appropriate
- link or instruction to bring clinical material to therapist
- instruction to bring legal questions to attorney

## Participant Dashboard Must Not Show

- Needs Attention flag
- therapist notes
- clinician review labels
- risk score
- relapse prediction
- compliance percentage
- success/failure rating
- court-benefit language
- attorney-facing language
- participation summary generation button
- spouse/partner access
- comparison to other participants
- automated accusation or escalation

## Weekly Module Flow

1. Show week title and orientation.
2. Show core teaching.
3. Show reality check.
4. Show legal/clinical boundary reminder.
5. Show reflection questions.
6. Show relationship/family reflection adapted by relationship status.
7. Show therapist prompt.
8. Ask for weekly integrity commitment.
9. Require completion checkbox.
10. Generate AI feedback after submission, under approved guardrails.

## Daily Check-In Flow

1. Show brief grounding statement.
2. Display Daily Check-In questions.
3. Require daily attestation checkbox.
4. Submit check-in.
5. Generate short AI feedback under approved guardrails.

## Relationship Adaptation

Participant onboarding should capture relationship status.

Relationship content should adapt for:

- married
- separated
- divorced
- cohabitating
- dating
- single
- no active partner

Weeks 17–20 are not skipped for single users. They adapt to family, child-safety, household rupture, community trust, and future relationship integrity.

## Language Rules

Use:

- sober
- direct
- non-shaming
- accountability-focused
- legally cautious
- clinically restrained

Avoid:

- court-performance language
- “prove change”
- “show the judge”
- “earn trust back”
- “complete this to help your case”
- “you are safe now”
- “you are healed”
- “you succeeded/failed”
