# Internal Review Checklist v1

Use this checklist before releasing any package, app feature, curriculum week, AI prompt, dashboard element, or professional material.

## Legal Boundary Review

- [ ] Does not provide legal advice
- [ ] Does not ask for offense details
- [ ] Does not ask for victim information
- [ ] Does not ask for investigative facts
- [ ] Does not ask for legal strategy
- [ ] Does not imply court benefit
- [ ] Does not create mitigation language
- [ ] Redirects legal matters to attorney

## Clinical Boundary Review

- [ ] Does not provide therapy
- [ ] Does not diagnose
- [ ] Does not interpret symptoms
- [ ] Does not assess risk
- [ ] Does not predict relapse
- [ ] Does not claim rehabilitation
- [ ] Redirects clinical concerns to licensed therapist/supervised provider

## Safety Review

- [ ] Includes crisis language where needed
- [ ] Does not minimize suicidal despair
- [ ] Does not intensify shame unnecessarily
- [ ] Does not imply app use is crisis care
- [ ] Directs immediate danger to 911/988

## Relationship Review

- [ ] Does not pressure spouse/partner forgiveness
- [ ] Does not make spouse/partner responsible for recovery
- [ ] Does not imply reconciliation is required
- [ ] Supports safety before closeness
- [ ] Adapts for single/non-partnered users

## App Ethics Review

- [ ] No surveillance
- [ ] No risk scoring
- [ ] No compliance percentage
- [ ] No success/failure dynamics
- [ ] No automated escalation
- [ ] Needs Attention remains clinician-facing curiosity only

## AI Review

- [ ] AI does not give legal advice
- [ ] AI does not give counseling advice
- [ ] AI does not summarize offense details
- [ ] AI does not determine progress
- [ ] AI does not create court language
- [ ] AI redirects crisis, legal, and clinical matters appropriately
