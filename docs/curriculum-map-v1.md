# The Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol

## 24-Week Curriculum Map v1

## Phase 1 — Stabilization After Exposure

### Week 1 — Orientation Intensive: The First 7 Days

Consolidating the initial crisis-orientation work into the first week: seriousness of the moment, stabilization, legal/recovery separation, role clarity, support boundaries, relationship responsibility, and a first concrete action plan without collecting offense details.

### Week 2 — Thinking Patterns and Daily Honesty

Identifying thinking errors, minimization, blame, secrecy, entitlement, self-pity, distorted self-talk, and thought-journal practices using present-tense, non-offense-detail language.

### Week 3 — Before You Act: Choice Points and Self-Control

Mapping the chain from vulnerability to thought, urge, opportunity, decision, and next-right action while practicing interruption, support use, environmental changes, and self-control without risk scoring or relapse prediction.

### Week 4 — Boundaries, Sexual Health, and Support Team

Defining healthy boundaries, non-explicit sexual health reflection, privacy versus secrecy, attorney/clinical/support roles, spouse/partner/family boundaries, and a concrete seven-day safeguard.

## Phase 2 — Truth, Shame, and Responsibility

### Week 5 — The Truth Problem

Facing secrecy, partial honesty, image management, hiding, and the internal system that allowed behavior to continue.

### Week 6 — Shame Without Collapse

Learning to face painful truth without self-hatred, self-pity, avoidance, or emotional shutdown.

### Week 7 — Responsibility Without Legal Recklessness

Taking ownership of patterns, choices, and recovery work while respecting attorney guidance and avoiding offense-detail disclosure inside the app.

### Week 8 — The Minimization Machine

Confronting distortions such as “it was only online,” “I never touched anyone,” “it was curiosity,” “I’m not like those men,” or “no one was really hurt.”

## Phase 3 — Pornography, Escalation, and Sexual Conditioning

### Week 9 — Adult Porn Was Part of the System

Connecting adult pornography, novelty seeking, fantasy, secrecy, emotional escape, objectification, and escalation.

### Week 10 — Escalation Does Not Happen All at Once

Understanding how tolerance, intensity, secrecy, and boundary drift develop over time.

### Week 11 — Fantasy, Objectification, and Disconnection

Examining how fantasy becomes a private world detached from empathy, intimacy, reality, and relational responsibility.

### Week 12 — The Boundary You Thought You Would Never Cross

Facing boundary progression without dramatizing, excusing, minimizing, or pretending it came out of nowhere.

## Phase 4 — Boundaries and Containment

### Week 13 — Digital Boundaries Are Recovery Boundaries

Addressing devices, apps, browsers, accounts, passwords, internet access, and environmental limits without turning the app into surveillance.

### Week 14 — Access, Opportunity, and Honesty

Reducing access to high-risk situations, contexts, platforms, routines, and isolation while remaining honest with appropriate people.

### Week 15 — No More Private Worlds

Ending secret accounts, secret devices, hidden routines, isolated emotional spaces, and private sexualized coping systems.

### Week 16 — Loneliness, Boredom, and Emotional Hunger

Connecting emotional loneliness, boredom, shame, disconnection, and sexual escape.

## Phase 5 — Marriage, Cohabitation, Family, and Relational Impact

### Week 17 — Facing Her Reality Without Defending Yourself

Learning to hear spouse/partner pain, fear, anger, grief, disgust, confusion, and mistrust without correction, self-pity, or pressure.

### Week 18 — Do Not Make Your Partner Carry Your Recovery

Avoiding the use of spouse/partner as therapist, probation officer, emotional regulator, accountability partner, or proof of change.

### Week 19 — Safety Before Reconciliation

Understanding that the first relational priority is not closeness, sex, forgiveness, or restored normalcy, but safety, truth, consistency, boundaries, and stability.

### Week 20 — Repair Without Demanding Hope

Practicing humility, patience, responsibility, and consistency without demanding forgiveness, reassurance, reconciliation, or emotional comfort.

## Phase 6 — Endurance and Future Integrity

### Week 21 — Living Under Legal Uncertainty

Continuing recovery while the legal, relational, vocational, financial, and family future remains unresolved.

### Week 22 — Preparing for Evaluation, Court, or Sentencing

Becoming grounded, honest, treatment-engaged, and appropriately prepared without rehearsing, performing, manipulating, or entering legal details into the app.

### Week 23 — The Man You Are Becoming

Clarifying identity, values, humility, accountability, sexuality, faith if relevant, relationships, and future integrity.

### Week 24 — A Long-Term Integrity Plan

Building the next 12 months of structure: therapy, group, digital boundaries, recovery supports, relational responsibility, crisis planning, and ongoing accountability.

## Bypass / Adaptation Rule

Weeks 17–20 are not skipped for single, divorced, or non-partnered participants. The app adapts those weeks from spouse/partner repair to family impact, household rupture, child-safety concerns, community trust, future relationship integrity, and relational responsibility.
