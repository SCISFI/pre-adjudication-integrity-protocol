# The Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol
## Track Charter v1

## 1. Official Name

**The Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol**

## 2. Subtitle

**A recovery-focused accountability program for men facing serious sexual behavior consequences**

## 3. Program Identity

The Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol is a specialized Integrity Protocol program for men facing serious consequences related to problematic sexual behavior before final legal resolution.

The program is designed for men who voluntarily want help, are willing to face the truth about their sexual behavior patterns, and are prepared to engage in structured accountability while also working with a licensed therapist or supervised graduate-level mental health intern.

## 4. Core Purpose

The purpose of the Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol is to help men remain honest, stable, accountable, sexually sober, legally compliant, relationally responsible, and treatment-engaged while navigating serious sexual behavior consequences and unresolved legal uncertainty.

The program does not exist to help a man avoid consequences.

It exists to help him become the kind of man who can face consequences with honesty, maturity, humility, and responsibility.

## 5. Core Clinical Premise

Legal involvement may be the crisis that exposes the problem, but the deeper work is sexual integrity.

Many participants will be dealing with the same patterns seen in broader problematic sexual behavior work, including secrecy, pornography dependence, escalation, fantasy, compartmentalization, shame, emotional avoidance, relational disconnection, loneliness, minimization, distorted thinking, and avoidance of accountability.

The program addresses these patterns without collecting offense details or attempting to determine guilt, legal responsibility, or risk.

## 6. Intended Participants

The Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol is designed for adult men who are facing serious sexual behavior consequences and who voluntarily want help.

Participants may include men who are:

- arrested, charged, investigated, accused, or awaiting adjudication related to problematic sexual behavior
- involved in CSAM/CSM-related legal concerns
- facing allegations or safety restrictions even when charges have not been filed
- asked or required to leave the home due to child-safety concerns
- dealing with adult pornography dependence, escalation, secrecy, fantasy, compulsive sexual behavior, or boundary violations
- married, separated, divorced, cohabitating, dating, or single
- willing to engage honestly in structured accountability
- willing to work with a licensed therapist outside the app

The protocol is not limited to men who have already been adjudicated. It is specifically designed for men whose legal, relational, family, or safety situation remains unresolved.

The program does not assume guilt, determine innocence, evaluate risk, or interpret legal facts. It focuses on sexual integrity, honesty, accountability, emotional stability, relational responsibility, and connection to appropriate professional help.

## 7. Exclusion Criteria and Higher-Level Review

The Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol is not appropriate as a stand-alone support for every man facing serious sexual behavior consequences.

A participant should be excluded, paused, or referred for higher-level clinical review if any of the following are present:

- active suicidal intent, plan, or inability to remain safe without immediate crisis support
- current intent to sexually offend, reoffend, contact a restricted person, violate safety restrictions, or access illegal sexual material
- refusal to comply with bond conditions, court orders, DSS/CPS requirements, attorney guidance, no-contact restrictions, device restrictions, or safety plans
- refusal to work with a licensed therapist
- participation primarily to influence the court, impress an attorney, satisfy a spouse/partner, or create the appearance of change
- severe psychiatric instability, substance use instability, cognitive impairment, psychosis, mania, or other condition requiring a higher level of care
- inability or unwillingness to avoid entering offense details, victim information, investigative facts, or legal strategy into the app
- use of the app as a substitute for therapy, forensic evaluation, legal counsel, crisis care, or mandated treatment
- minor status as a direct participant

When exclusion concerns are present, the appropriate response is not punishment or shame. The appropriate response is referral to the correct level of human care, legal guidance, crisis support, or clinical oversight.

The protocol may support a man’s accountability work, but it must not be used when the app would create false safety, false treatment, false documentation, or false confidence.

## 8. Licensed Therapist / Supervised Intern Requirement

Participation in the Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol requires involvement with a licensed therapist or a qualified graduate-level mental health intern working under the supervision of a fully licensed supervisor therapist.

This protocol is not designed to function as stand-alone care. The men using this program may be facing significant shame, legal uncertainty, family disruption, suicidal despair, sexual behavior escalation, trauma history, compulsive behavior patterns, and relational fallout. These issues require qualified human clinical support.

The app may provide structure, reflection, accountability, and continuity between sessions, but it does not replace therapy, forensic evaluation, legal counsel, crisis care, or mandated treatment.

Participants must agree that offense-related details, legal concerns, risk-related concerns, trauma history, psychiatric instability, suicidal thoughts, and deeper clinical material belong with their licensed therapist, supervised graduate-level intern, clinical supervisor, and/or attorney — not inside the app.

### Required Participant Attestation

Before beginning the program, each participant must acknowledge:

> I understand that the Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol does not replace therapy, legal counsel, forensic evaluation, crisis care, or mandated treatment. I agree to work with a licensed therapist or a supervised graduate-level mental health intern where clinical details, offense-related issues, risk-related concerns, emotional instability, and deeper recovery work can be addressed appropriately.

### Clinician Access Standard

Participant reflections may be reviewed by an assigned licensed clinician, a qualified graduate-level mental health intern under supervision, or an approved clinical administrator.

Reflections should not be assigned to a peer mentor, sponsor, recovery coach, pastor, attorney, spouse/partner, or non-clinical accountability partner because of the sensitivity and legal/clinical risk of this population.

### Supervision Requirement for Interns

Graduate-level mental health interns may participate in the program only when they are formally supervised by a fully licensed supervisor therapist who remains clinically responsible for oversight, consultation, and review.

## 9. No-Offense-Detail Policy

The Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol must not become a confession record, investigative record, legal strategy record, or substitute clinical file.

Participants must not enter offense details into the app.

The app should not ask for, invite, summarize, interpret, or store details about alleged or actual illegal sexual behavior.

This includes, but is not limited to:

- victim names
- child names
- identifying information about alleged victims
- offense narratives
- police report details
- investigative facts
- file, image, or video descriptions
- CSAM/CSM content descriptions
- search terms used to locate illegal material
- device details related to evidence
- dates, locations, or timelines of alleged offenses
- confession narratives
- legal strategy
- attorney-client privileged information
- court testimony preparation
- details about charges beyond basic participant context

### Approved Focus of App Reflections

The app may ask about:

- honesty
- secrecy
- minimization
- shame
- emotional avoidance
- adult pornography patterns
- escalation awareness
- digital boundaries
- relational impact
- accountability
- support systems
- legal and safety compliance in general terms
- weekly integrity commitments
- what needs to be discussed with the participant’s licensed therapist or attorney

### Participant Warning Language

Before reflection entry, the app should display:

> Do not enter offense details, victim names, investigative facts, illegal content descriptions, police facts, or legal strategy. Use this space for recovery reflection, accountability, boundaries, emotions, and next right actions. Discuss legal details with your attorney and clinical details with your licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider.

### If a Participant Begins Entering Offense Details

The app should not attempt to interpret the content, classify the content, or determine legal risk.

The proper response is a clear written boundary:

> This app is not the place to record offense details, victim information, investigative facts, illegal content descriptions, or legal strategy. Please remove those details and discuss them with your attorney and licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider.

The app should redirect the participant back to recovery-focused reflection.

## 10. Legal Disclaimer and Legal Boundaries

The Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol does not provide legal advice.

Participants must rely on their attorney for legal guidance, including questions related to charges, investigations, disclosure, evidence, court expectations, plea decisions, testimony, bond conditions, sentencing, probation, registration, contact restrictions, technology restrictions, and any other legal matter.

The app must not instruct participants on what to say to law enforcement, attorneys, evaluators, courts, solicitors, case workers, spouses/partners, or family members regarding alleged or actual offense details.

The program may encourage participants to remain legally compliant, honest with appropriate professionals, and accountable to court-ordered or attorney-directed restrictions. However, it must not interpret legal obligations, provide legal strategy, or advise participants on how to manage their case.

### Participant-Facing Legal Boundary Statement

Participants should see this language during onboarding:

> This program is not legal advice. Do not use this app to record legal strategy, offense details, investigative facts, or attorney-client privileged information. Bring legal questions to your attorney. Follow all court orders, bond conditions, safety restrictions, and attorney guidance.

### Professional-Facing Legal Boundary Statement

For attorneys, case workers, or court-related professionals:

> The Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol is a structured recovery-accountability program. It does not determine guilt or innocence, assess legal responsibility, evaluate forensic risk, provide legal advice, or make recommendations regarding adjudication, sentencing, probation, registration, custody, or contact restrictions.

### Court-Outcome Boundary

The program must never promise, imply, or market that participation will improve a participant’s legal outcome.

Participant-facing materials must not say or imply:

- this will help your case
- this will impress the judge
- this will influence the solicitor
- this will reduce consequences
- this proves rehabilitation
- this demonstrates low risk
- this substitutes for treatment
- this satisfies legal requirements unless specifically ordered by a court or approved professional

### Approved Legal-Context Language

The program may say:

> This protocol helps you remain structured, honest, accountable, treatment-engaged, and legally compliant while your legal situation remains unresolved.

## 11. Clinical Disclaimer and Clinical Boundaries

The Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol is not therapy, counseling, crisis care, forensic evaluation, psychosexual evaluation, or clinical treatment.

The protocol provides structured reflection, accountability support, recovery-focused education, and continuity between professional sessions. It is designed to support clinical work, not replace it.

Participants must work with a licensed therapist or a supervised graduate-level mental health intern where deeper clinical issues can be addressed appropriately.

Clinical matters that belong with a licensed therapist, supervised clinical provider, or clinical supervisor include:

- offense-related clinical details
- trauma history
- suicidal thoughts
- psychiatric instability
- compulsive sexual behavior patterns
- sexual behavior escalation
- shame collapse
- substance use concerns
- family and partner crisis
- risk-related concerns
- mandated treatment requirements
- disclosure planning
- relapse prevention planning
- safety planning

The app may help participants identify what they need to bring to therapy, but it must not provide therapy, diagnose, interpret symptoms, evaluate risk, or determine treatment progress.

### Participant-Facing Clinical Boundary Statement

Participants should see this language during onboarding:

> This program is not therapy and does not replace working with a licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider. Use this program for structured reflection, accountability, and weekly integrity work. Bring clinical details, offense-related issues, emotional instability, suicidal thoughts, safety concerns, and deeper recovery work to your therapist or supervised clinical provider.

### Professional-Facing Clinical Boundary Statement

For clinicians, attorneys, case workers, or court-related professionals:

> The Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol is a structured accountability and recovery-support program. It does not provide clinical treatment, diagnosis, psychosexual evaluation, forensic evaluation, risk assessment, treatment discharge, or clinical opinion regarding safety, risk, rehabilitation, or legal responsibility.

## 12. Motivation Standard

Participants should not enter the program primarily to impress a court, attorney, spouse, case worker, solicitor, or judge.

The proper motivation is:

> I am doing this because integrity requires it, whether or not it helps my legal situation.

## 13. Relationship to Integrity Protocol

The Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol is a specialized Integrity Protocol program. It shares the same larger values of honesty, accountability, sexual integrity, and structured reflection, but it is intentionally separated because of the legal, relational, and clinical sensitivity of this population.

It should be built in a separate private repository and presented through a discreet program identity.

## 14. Foundational Boundary

The app may support clinical accountability, but clinical judgment remains with the licensed therapist, supervised clinical provider, and clinical supervisor.

The app may support integrity during legal uncertainty, but it must not become a legal tool, legal record, sentencing aid, forensic opinion, or mitigation product.

The app exists to support accountability and recovery structure. It does not exist to collect evidence, evaluate truthfulness, document confession, assess risk, advise on legal disclosure, or determine treatment progress.

The protocol holds structure.

People hold meaning.
