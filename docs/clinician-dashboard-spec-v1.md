# Clinician Dashboard Specification v1

## Purpose

The clinician dashboard exists to support human review of assigned participants.

It must not become a surveillance system, risk scoring tool, relapse prediction system, legal monitoring tool, or automated intervention system.

## Authorized Viewers

Dashboard access may be granted to:

- assigned licensed clinician
- supervised graduate-level mental health intern under supervision
- clinical supervisor
- approved clinical administrator

Dashboard access should not be granted to:

- spouse/partner
- attorney
- pastor
- peer mentor
- sponsor
- court
- case worker
- employer
- family member

## Participant List View

The dashboard may display:

- participant name
- current week
- program status
- last activity date
- weekly reflection submitted/not submitted
- daily check-in submitted/not submitted
- Needs Attention status
- assigned clinician/supervised provider

## Sorting

Needs Attention should sort first.

## Needs Attention Meaning

Needs Attention means clinician curiosity, not participant failure.

It may be triggered by inactivity but must not imply risk, relapse, noncompliance, or legal concern.

## Participant Detail View

The participant detail view may include:

1. Header
2. Progress overview by week: submitted / not submitted only
3. Most recent weekly reflection
4. Daily check-in history
5. AI feedback history
6. Clinician notes if added later
7. Participation Summary generation area
8. Needs Attention flag

## What the Dashboard Must Not Include

The dashboard must not include:

- risk score
- relapse prediction
- legal-risk label
- clinical diagnosis generated by app
- safety rating
- success/failure rating
- compliance percentage
- automated accusation
- automated recommendation
- court outcome implication
- spouse/partner messaging
- participant comparison analytics

## Daily Check-In Display

The dashboard may show daily check-in content, including HALT-BS selections, sleep, movement, isolation, strongest emotion, vulnerability level, boundary, support contact, therapist follow-up, and daily commitment.

These must be presented as self-attested information only, not interpreted risk.

## Participation Summary Access

Participation Summary generation should be restricted to licensed clinicians, supervised providers with supervisor approval, or approved clinical administrators.

The summary must document participation facts only.
