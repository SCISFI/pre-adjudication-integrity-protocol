# Weekly Module Standard v1

## Purpose

Weekly modules in The Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol are structured recovery-accountability assignments. They are not brief reading pages, therapy sessions, legal exercises, forensic evaluations, risk assessments, relapse predictions, or court-performance documents.

## Time Standard

Each weekly module should be designed for approximately 30 minutes of focused work, not counting daily check-ins.

## Required Module Pattern

Each weekly module should include:

1. structured teaching that names the weekly theme in a firm, sober, non-shaming tone;
2. clear no-offense-detail boundaries;
3. separate written response fields rather than one general reflection box;
4. prompts that direct legal details to the participant's attorney;
5. prompts that direct clinical material to a licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider;
6. a relationship, family, household, or future-relationship responsibility prompt that does not pressure a spouse or partner for reassurance;
7. a concrete weekly action plan or next-right-action prompt;
8. a weekly integrity commitment that is behavioral and limited to the next 7 days;
9. a completion attestation.

## Boundary Standard

Weekly modules must not collect offense details, victim names, investigative facts, illegal content descriptions, police facts, or legal strategy. The app should use this space for recovery reflection, accountability, boundaries, emotions, support, and next right actions.

## Review / Self-Inventory Standard

Self-inventory items may help participants notice areas such as isolation, sleep disruption, shame intensity, panic, image management, or avoidance of therapy/support. These items must remain self-attestation only. They must not be scored, interpreted as risk, treated as relapse prediction, or presented as success/failure language.

## Clinician Display Standard

Clinicians may review participant responses as self-attested written content. The app should display each prompt and answer clearly. The app must not summarize, interpret, score, diagnose, predict risk, recommend legal action, or state that a participant is safe or rehabilitated.


## Phase 1 Initial Module Themes

The initial Weeks 1-4 module sequence should follow this standard:

1. Week 1: The Day Everything Changed
2. Week 2: Staying Alive and Grounded
3. Week 3: Legal Trouble Is Not Recovery
4. Week 4: Who Belongs Where
