# Daily Check-In Specification v1

## Purpose

The Daily Check-In helps the participant pause, tell the truth, notice vulnerability, and remain connected to his integrity commitment.

The check-in is not a risk assessment, surveillance system, relapse prediction tool, compliance score, or substitute for therapy.

## Daily Check-In Items

### 1. HALT-BS Vulnerability Check

Question:

> Which of these are affecting me today?

Options:

- Hungry
- Angry
- Lonely
- Tired
- Bored
- Stressed
- None of these

### 2. Sleep

Question:

> How was my sleep last night?

Options:

- Restful
- Okay
- Poor
- Very poor
- I did not sleep

### 3. Exercise / Movement

Question:

> Did I move my body or exercise today?

Options:

- Yes
- No
- Not yet, but I plan to

### 4. Isolation

Question:

> Have I isolated today?

Options:

- No
- Somewhat
- Yes

### 5. Emotional Condition

Question:

> What emotion is strongest in me today?

Options:

- Shame
- Fear
- Anger
- Sadness
- Numbness
- Anxiety
- Loneliness
- Hope
- Other

Optional short text:

> If other, name it briefly.

### 6. Secrecy / Escape Vulnerability

Question:

> How vulnerable am I today to secrecy, fantasy, pornography, sexual escape, or emotional shutdown?

Options:

- Low
- Moderate
- High

This is self-attestation only. It must not be scored, labeled, or interpreted as risk.

### 7. Boundaries

Question:

> What boundary do I need to honor in the next 24 hours?

Short text response.

### 8. Support Contact

Question:

> Have I had honest contact with a safe support person today?

Options:

- Yes
- No
- Not yet, but I need to

### 9. Therapist Follow-Up

Question:

> Is there anything from today I need to bring to my licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider?

Options:

- Yes
- No

Optional short text:

> Briefly name the topic without entering offense details.

### 10. Daily Integrity Commitment

Question:

> What is one right action I will take before the day ends?

Short text response.

### 11. Daily Attestation Checkbox

Required wording:

> I completed this check-in honestly and without intentionally hiding what needs attention.

## Clinician Visibility

Daily check-ins may be visible to the assigned clinician, supervised intern, clinical supervisor, or approved clinical administrator.

They should not be visible to spouse/partner, attorney, pastor, peer mentor, sponsor, court, or case worker unless later reflected only as participation facts in an authorized summary.

## Dashboard Display

The clinician dashboard may show:

- last check-in date
- check-in submitted / not submitted
- selected vulnerability items
- written boundary
- daily commitment
- therapist follow-up marked yes/no

The dashboard must not show risk score, relapse prediction, compliance percentage, success/failure rating, automated warning label, or “high risk” status.

## Missed Check-Ins

Missed daily check-ins must not trigger shame language, automated alerts, or failure labels. They may update last activity date and submitted/not submitted status only.
