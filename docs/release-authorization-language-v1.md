# Release / Authorization Language v1

## Purpose

A Participation Summary may be shared only with written participant authorization.

The authorization should specify the recipient, purpose, date range, and type of information to be released.

## Authorization Statement

> I authorize The Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol / South Carolina Institute for Sexual Integrity to release a Participation Summary documenting my structured participation activity in the program.

## Recipient

Authorized recipient:

- Name: [Recipient Name]
- Role/Organization: [Attorney / Licensed Therapist / Supervised Provider / Case Worker / Evaluator / Court-Related Professional / Other]
- Email or Delivery Method: [Contact Information]

## Authorized Information

The Participation Summary may include:

- participant name
- program name
- enrollment date
- current program status
- weeks available
- weeks completed
- weekly reflections submitted: yes/no or count
- weekly integrity commitments submitted: yes/no or count
- completion attestations submitted: yes/no or count
- daily check-ins submitted: count or date range
- last activity date
- assigned clinician or supervising clinical provider name, if appropriate
- administrative note, if needed

## Information Not Authorized for Release

The Participation Summary must not include:

- reflection answers
- daily check-in answers
- AI feedback
- offense details
- alleged victim information
- investigative facts
- legal strategy
- clinical interpretation
- diagnosis
- risk opinion
- relapse prediction
- claim of rehabilitation
- statement of safety
- legal recommendation
- sentencing recommendation
- custody/contact recommendation
- guilt or innocence statement

## Required Acknowledgment

> I understand that the Participation Summary documents activity only. It does not provide legal, forensic, clinical, risk, treatment, sentencing, custody, contact, or rehabilitation conclusions.
