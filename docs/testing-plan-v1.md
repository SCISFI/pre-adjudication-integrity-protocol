# Testing Plan v1

## Purpose

The first test group is designed to evaluate clarity, safety, usability, emotional tone, boundary protection, AI feedback containment, and clinician usefulness.

Testing is not a clinical outcome study and must not be described as proving effectiveness, safety, rehabilitation, or risk reduction.

## First Test Group

Recommended test group:

- 3–5 adult men
- clinically known to the program owner or clinician
- voluntarily seeking help
- stable enough for app-based reflection
- working with a licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider
- not acutely suicidal without crisis support
- not using the app as a replacement for therapy or legal counsel

## Test Content

Initial test content should include:

- onboarding acknowledgments
- daily check-in
- weeks 1–4
- AI feedback
- participant dashboard
- clinician dashboard
- Needs Attention logic
- participation summary prototype

Weeks 5–24 may exist in the repo but should not be released to test users until the initial workflow is stable.

## Participant Feedback Questions

Ask after each week:

1. Was the weekly content clear?
2. Did anything feel confusing?
3. Did any prompt feel legally unsafe?
4. Did any question invite details you should not type into the app?
5. Did the tone feel firm without being shaming?
6. Did the AI feedback feel helpful?
7. Did the AI feedback feel like legal advice or therapy?
8. Did the daily check-in help you notice vulnerability?
9. Did the completion checkbox feel clear?
10. What should be changed before more users are added?

## Clinician Feedback Questions

Ask clinicians/admin after review:

1. Was the dashboard clinically useful without becoming interpretive?
2. Was too much information shown?
3. Was anything important missing?
4. Did the app avoid risk scoring and legal interpretation?
5. Did AI feedback remain within guardrails?
6. Would the Participation Summary be appropriate if authorized?
7. Did the daily check-in create useful visibility without surveillance?

## Stop Conditions

Pause testing if:

- users enter repeated offense details despite warnings
- AI feedback gives legal or counseling advice
- any language appears to encourage court performance
- users appear to treat the app as a therapy substitute
- crisis language appears insufficient
- clinician reviewers identify legal or clinical risk
- spouse/partner content appears to pressure reconciliation

## Expansion Rule

Do not expand beyond 3–5 users until Weeks 1–4, daily check-ins, AI feedback, dashboard display, and participation summary prototype are reviewed and revised.
