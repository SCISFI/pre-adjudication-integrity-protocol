# Participation Summary Rules v1

## Purpose

The Participation Summary documents structured participation in The Pre-Adjudication Integrity Protocol.

It may show that a participant engaged with weekly recovery reflections, daily check-ins, integrity commitments, and completion attestations.

It does not prove change, determine risk, verify truthfulness, provide legal opinion, provide clinical opinion, summarize offense details, or recommend legal outcomes.

Its purpose is to document participation in structured accountability work.

## Authorized Recipients

A Participation Summary may be shared only with written participant authorization.

Possible recipients:

- participant
- attorney
- licensed therapist
- supervised clinical provider
- case worker
- evaluator
- court-related professional
- probation/parole professional if relevant later
- other professional authorized by the participant

The summary should not be shared with spouse/partner, family member, pastor, employer, or non-clinical mentor unless specifically reviewed and approved as clinically and legally appropriate.

## May Include

- participant name
- program name
- enrollment date
- current program status
- weeks available
- weeks completed
- weekly reflections submitted: yes/no
- weekly integrity commitments submitted: yes/no
- completion attestations submitted: yes/no
- daily check-ins submitted: count or date range
- last activity date
- assigned clinician or supervising clinical provider name, if appropriate
- administrative note, if needed

## Must Not Include

- reflection answers
- daily check-in answers
- AI feedback
- offense details
- alleged victim information
- legal facts
- investigative details
- clinical interpretations
- diagnostic impressions
- risk opinions
- relapse predictions
- claims of rehabilitation
- statements that the participant is safe
- statements that the participant is low risk
- statements that the participant has completed treatment
- sentencing recommendations
- court recommendations
- custody/contact recommendations
- statements about guilt or innocence

## Required Disclaimer

Every Participation Summary must include:

> This summary documents participation only. It is not a forensic evaluation, psychosexual evaluation, risk assessment, legal opinion, clinical treatment summary, treatment discharge summary, or sentencing recommendation. It does not determine guilt or innocence, verify truthfulness, assess safety, establish rehabilitation, or recommend any legal outcome.

## Summary Access Rule

Participation Summary generation should be restricted to licensed clinicians, supervised clinical providers with supervisor approval, or approved clinical administrators.

Participants should not self-generate official summaries without administrative review.
