# Week 2 — Staying Alive and Grounded

## Orientation

This week is about survival, grounding, and connection.

Serious sexual behavior consequences can create despair, panic, shame, and thoughts of disappearing. The first responsibility is to stay alive, stay connected, and bring the truth to appropriate human support.

## Crisis Safety Statement

This program is not a crisis service.

If you are in immediate danger or believe you may harm yourself or someone else, call 911 or 988 now. Do not remain alone. Contact your licensed therapist, attorney-approved support person, sponsor, pastor, or emergency contact.

## Core Teaching

Shame can become dangerous when it turns inward and isolates you.

A man in crisis may begin to believe:

- “My life is over.”
- “Everyone would be better without me.”
- “I cannot face this.”
- “There is no future.”
- “If people knew, I would be destroyed.”
- “I have already lost everything.”

These thoughts may feel convincing, but they are not reliable leadership.

Your task is not to solve the next year today. Your task is to remain alive and grounded for the next right step.

Grounding is not denial. It is the discipline of staying present long enough to act with responsibility.

## Reality Check

Isolation is not privacy. Isolation is danger.

Panic is not repentance. Panic is a nervous system state.

Shame is not accountability. Shame often becomes avoidance.

The work this week is to stop treating despair as truth and start treating it as a signal that you need support.

## Legal and Clinical Boundary Reminder

Do not enter offense details, victim names, investigative facts, illegal content descriptions, police facts, or legal strategy. Use this space for recovery reflection, accountability, boundaries, emotions, and next right actions. Discuss legal details with your attorney and clinical details with your licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider.

## Reflection Questions

1. What truth am I facing this week?
2. Where am I tempted to hide, minimize, blame, or manage my image?
3. What emotion is most dangerous for me right now?
4. What boundary do I need to honor this week?
5. Who needs to know how I am really doing?
6. What do I need to discuss with my licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider?
7. What is my one integrity commitment this week?

## Relationship / Family Reflection

If you have a spouse or partner, do not use her fear or pain as proof that you should collapse. Her response belongs to her. Your responsibility is to become stable enough to respect her boundaries and seek appropriate support elsewhere.

If you are not partnered, identify who in your life can know that you are struggling without needing to know legal or offense details. You need appropriate human connection.

## Therapist Prompt

Bring any suicidal thoughts, despair, panic, sleep disruption, inability to function, or fear of being alone to your licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider immediately.
