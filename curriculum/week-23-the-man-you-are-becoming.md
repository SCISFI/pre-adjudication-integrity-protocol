# Week 23 — The Man You Are Becoming

## Orientation

This week is about identity.

The goal is not to create a positive self-image, deny consequences, or declare yourself changed. The goal is to examine the man you are becoming through repeated choices, boundaries, honesty, humility, and accountability.

## Core Teaching

A sexual behavior crisis can shatter identity.

A man may feel trapped between two false identities:

- “I am a monster.”
- “This is not really me.”

Neither is enough.

If he says only, “I am a monster,” he may collapse in shame and avoid responsibility.

If he says only, “This is not really me,” he may avoid facing the patterns that were part of his life.

Integrity requires a harder truth:

> I am responsible for what I have allowed, hidden, fed, minimized, and practiced. I am also responsible for the man I am becoming now.

Identity is not built by statements alone. It is built through repeated practice.

The man you are becoming is shaped by:

- what you tell the truth about
- what you stop protecting
- what boundaries you honor
- how you respond to shame
- how you handle loneliness
- who you allow to know you
- whether you seek clinical help
- whether you respect legal and safety restrictions
- how you respond to those you have harmed
- whether you keep doing the work when no one praises you

This week asks you to look forward without pretending the past does not matter.

## Reality Check

You do not become a man of integrity by calling yourself one.

You do not erase harm by building a better identity.

You do not prove change by wanting to be different.

You become different through repeated truth, repeated boundaries, repeated humility, and repeated accountability.

## Reflection Questions

1. What truth am I facing this week?
2. Where am I tempted to hide, minimize, blame, or manage my image?
3. What emotion is most dangerous for me right now?
4. What boundary do I need to honor this week?
5. Who needs to know how I am really doing?
6. What do I need to discuss with my licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider?
7. What is my one integrity commitment this week?

## Relationship / Family Reflection

If you have a spouse or partner, do not ask her to accept your new identity because you want it to be true. Let your work become consistent without pressuring her conclusion.

If you are single or not partnered, consider what kind of man you would need to become before future intimacy, family trust, or community trust could be approached with humility.
