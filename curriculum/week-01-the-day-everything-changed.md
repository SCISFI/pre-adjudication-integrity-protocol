# Week 1 — The Day Everything Changed

## Orientation

This week is about staying grounded after exposure, accusation, arrest, removal from the home, or legal crisis.

The goal is not to explain everything, fix everything, or control the outcome. The goal is to stay alive, honest, connected, legally compliant, and accountable.

## Core Teaching

A serious sexual behavior crisis can collapse a man’s normal way of functioning. In one day, privacy may disappear, family stability may rupture, legal consequences may begin, and the man may feel exposed in ways he never imagined.

This week does not ask you to explain the whole story. It does not ask you to type offense details. It does not ask you to defend yourself, condemn yourself, or predict what will happen.

It asks you to do the first honest work: stop running.

When a man is exposed, arrested, accused, or removed from the home, he often moves toward one of several reactions:

- panic
- secrecy
- image management
- blame
- self-pity
- collapse
- desperate promises
- emotional shutdown
- suicidal despair
- attempts to control what others think

None of those reactions produce integrity.

Integrity begins with a smaller step: telling the truth about your current condition and choosing the next right action without making things worse.

## Reality Check

You may want to explain, defend, minimize, or make someone understand your side.

You may want to tell yourself, “This is not who I am.”

You may want to focus only on what will happen legally, financially, relationally, or publicly.

Those concerns may be real, but they are not the center of this week’s work.

This week’s work is to remain grounded enough to tell the truth, follow boundaries, stay connected to the right people, and not use panic as an excuse to hide.

## Legal and Clinical Boundary Reminder

Do not enter offense details, victim names, investigative facts, illegal content descriptions, police facts, or legal strategy. Use this space for recovery reflection, accountability, boundaries, emotions, and next right actions. Discuss legal details with your attorney and clinical details with your licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider.

## Reflection Questions

1. What truth am I facing this week?
2. Where am I tempted to hide, minimize, blame, or manage my image?
3. What emotion is most dangerous for me right now?
4. What boundary do I need to honor this week?
5. Who needs to know how I am really doing?
6. What do I need to discuss with my licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider?
7. What is my one integrity commitment this week?

## Relationship / Family Reflection

If you are married, separated, cohabitating, dating, or recently removed from the home, do not make your spouse or partner responsible for calming you down. Her reaction may include fear, anger, grief, disgust, confusion, numbness, or distance.

Your work this week is not to secure reassurance.

Your work is to become stable, truthful, and appropriately supported.

If you are single or not in an active relationship, consider the family, household, children, community, or future relationships affected by your behavior, accusation, or legal crisis. What would it look like to become safer, steadier, and more honest without demanding trust from anyone?

## Therapist Prompt

Bring your strongest emotion, your most dangerous avoidance pattern, and your most urgent support need to your licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider.

Do not use the app as the place to process details that belong in clinical care or legal counsel.
