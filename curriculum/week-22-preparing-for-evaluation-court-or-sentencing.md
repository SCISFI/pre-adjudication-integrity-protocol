# Week 22 — Preparing for Evaluation, Court, or Sentencing

## Orientation

This week is about preparing responsibly without performing.

The goal is to become grounded, honest, treatment-engaged, and appropriately supported while respecting legal boundaries and avoiding court-performance language.

## Core Teaching

As legal events approach, a man may become focused on how he will be seen.

He may think about:

- what the judge will think
- what the solicitor will think
- what the evaluator will think
- what the attorney needs
- what his spouse or family may say
- how to sound remorseful
- how to explain himself
- whether his recovery work will matter
- whether the future will be destroyed

Some preparation is appropriate. But performance is dangerous.

Performance asks, “How do I sound changed?”

Integrity asks, “Am I practicing truth when no one is watching?”

This program must not be used to rehearse legal statements, prepare testimony, write sentencing language, or construct mitigation arguments. Legal preparation belongs with your attorney. Clinical preparation belongs with your licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider.

Your work here is different. It is to remain grounded, accountable, and honest about your patterns, commitments, supports, and boundaries.

## Reality Check

Do not confuse looking prepared with being responsible.

Do not confuse saying the right words with becoming honest.

Do not use this program to create language for court.

Do not turn recovery into a performance.

The legal system may consider many things. Your job in this protocol is not to manage that. Your job is to keep doing the work.

## Legal and Clinical Boundary Reminder

Do not enter offense details, victim names, investigative facts, illegal content descriptions, police facts, or legal strategy. Use this space for recovery reflection, accountability, boundaries, emotions, and next right actions. Discuss legal details with your attorney and clinical details with your licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider.

## Reflection Questions

1. What truth am I facing this week?
2. Where am I tempted to hide, minimize, blame, or manage my image?
3. What emotion is most dangerous for me right now?
4. What boundary do I need to honor this week?
5. Who needs to know how I am really doing?
6. What do I need to discuss with my licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider?
7. What is my one integrity commitment this week?

## Relationship / Family Reflection

If you have a spouse or partner, do not ask her to support your image or manage how others perceive you. She gets to have her own truth, grief, boundaries, and process.

If you are single or not partnered, identify where you may be tempted to perform recovery for family, church, employer, court, or community rather than practice integrity consistently.

## Therapist Prompt

Bring this question to therapy:

> Where am I tempted to perform change instead of practicing accountability?
