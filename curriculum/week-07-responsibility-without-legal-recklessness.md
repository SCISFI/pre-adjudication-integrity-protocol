# Week 7 — Responsibility Without Legal Recklessness

## Orientation

This week is about learning the difference between recovery responsibility and legal recklessness.

You can take ownership of patterns, choices, avoidance, secrecy, and recovery work without using this app to record legal facts or offense details.

## Core Teaching

Some men avoid responsibility by saying, “My attorney told me not to talk.”

Other men become reckless by trying to prove honesty in the wrong place, with the wrong people, or in writing where it does not belong.

Both extremes are dangerous.

Legal caution does not excuse recovery avoidance.

Recovery honesty does not require putting offense details in this app.

Responsibility in this program means facing the patterns that belong to recovery:

- secrecy
- pornography use
- fantasy
- entitlement
- emotional avoidance
- minimization
- isolation
- boundary drift
- dishonesty
- failure to seek help
- failure to honor restrictions
- failure to tell the truth to the right professionals

Legal details belong with your attorney. Deeper clinical and offense-related material belongs with your licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider.

The app belongs to structured recovery reflection.

## Reality Check

Do not use legal caution as a shield against honesty.

Do not use honesty as an excuse to be reckless.

Do not write details here that belong elsewhere.

Do not confuse silence with integrity.

Responsibility is not saying everything everywhere. Responsibility is bringing the right truth to the right place with the right person.

## Legal and Clinical Boundary Reminder

Do not enter offense details, victim names, investigative facts, illegal content descriptions, police facts, or legal strategy. Use this space for recovery reflection, accountability, boundaries, emotions, and next right actions. Discuss legal details with your attorney and clinical details with your licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider.

## Reflection Questions

1. What truth am I facing this week?
2. Where am I tempted to hide, minimize, blame, or manage my image?
3. What emotion is most dangerous for me right now?
4. What boundary do I need to honor this week?
5. Who needs to know how I am really doing?
6. What do I need to discuss with my licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider?
7. What is my one integrity commitment this week?

## Relationship / Family Reflection

If your spouse or partner asks questions you cannot or should not answer outside attorney/clinical guidance, do not lie, attack, blame, or manipulate.

A responsible response may be:

> I want to be honest, and I also need to handle legal and clinical details in the right setting. I will not use that as an excuse to avoid recovery work.

If you are single or not partnered, consider where you have used privacy, legal caution, or embarrassment to avoid appropriate accountability.

## Therapist Prompt

Bring this question to therapy:

> What does responsibility look like for me this week without entering legal or offense details into unsafe places?

## Weekly Integrity Commitment

What is one concrete responsibility action you will take this week that respects both recovery honesty and legal boundaries?
