# Week 6 — Shame Without Collapse

## Orientation

This week is about facing shame without letting shame become collapse, self-hatred, self-pity, or avoidance.

The goal is not to feel better quickly. The goal is to remain present, honest, and responsible while shame is active.

## Core Teaching

Shame often says, “I am beyond repair.”

Responsibility says, “I must face what is true and do the next right thing.”

Those are not the same.

Many men confuse shame with accountability. They believe that if they hate themselves enough, they are being honest. But shame can become another hiding place. It can turn attention away from harm, responsibility, and change, and focus everything on how unbearable the man feels.

Shame may show up as:

- “I am disgusting.”
- “There is no point.”
- “I have ruined everything.”
- “No one could ever know me now.”
- “I deserve whatever happens.”
- “I cannot look at myself.”
- “I just want to disappear.”
- “I am the worst thing I have done or been accused of.”

Some of these statements may feel emotionally true, but they do not produce integrity by themselves.

A man can collapse in shame and still not become honest.

A man can cry and still avoid responsibility.

A man can punish himself emotionally and still not change his patterns.

The work is to let shame tell you something is deeply wrong without letting shame become your master.

## Crisis Safety Statement

This program is not a crisis service.

If you are in immediate danger or believe you may harm yourself or someone else, call 911 or 988 now. Do not remain alone. Contact your licensed therapist, attorney-approved support person, sponsor, pastor, or emergency contact.

## Reality Check

Shame is not payment.

Self-hatred is not repair.

Despair is not truth.

Collapse does not make you safer.

The more serious the consequences, the more important it is to stay grounded enough to be responsible.

## Reflection Questions

1. What truth am I facing this week?
2. Where am I tempted to hide, minimize, blame, or manage my image?
3. What emotion is most dangerous for me right now?
4. What boundary do I need to honor this week?
5. Who needs to know how I am really doing?
6. What do I need to discuss with my licensed therapist or supervised clinical provider?
7. What is my one integrity commitment this week?

## Relationship / Family Reflection

If you have a spouse or partner, do not use shame to ask her to rescue you. Her pain is not an emergency service for your shame.

If you are single or not partnered, identify one safe, appropriate person who can know that shame is active without needing to know legal or offense details.

## Therapist Prompt

Bring this question to therapy:

> When shame becomes intense, do I collapse, hide, seek reassurance, become defensive, or become numb?

## Weekly Integrity Commitment
