# Week 14 — Access, Opportunity, and Honesty

## Orientation

This week is about access and opportunity.

The goal is to reduce the conditions that make secrecy, fantasy, pornography, sexual escape, boundary violations, or isolation easier to protect.

## Core Teaching

Recovery is not only about desire. It is also about access.

A man may sincerely want to change while still leaving himself surrounded by opportunities to hide. That is not wisdom. That is divided living.

Access includes more than devices. It may include:

- private time
- isolated routines
- unstructured evenings
- unsupervised internet access
- old accounts
- emotional secrecy
- travel patterns
- workplace loopholes
- private spending
- substance use
- unaccountable friendships
- hidden resentment
- fantasy rituals
- rooms, places, or situations connected to secrecy

Opportunity is what happens when access meets vulnerability.

Honesty asks a different question:

> Where am I leaving myself too much room to become the man I said I do not want to be?

Reducing access is not about proving you are weak. It is about becoming honest enough to stop trusting the old system.

## Reality Check

If you only remove access after you are caught, you are reacting.

If you remove access because you understand your pattern, you are practicing integrity.

If you keep private opportunities because “nothing happened this time,” you are still negotiating with the system that harmed you and others.

## Relationship / Family Reflection

If you have a spouse or partner, safety will not be rebuilt by promises alone. It will be affected by whether you willingly reduce access, opportunity, secrecy, and loopholes without making her police you.

If you are single or not partnered, identify the access and opportunity patterns that would make future integrity weak before a relationship even begins.
